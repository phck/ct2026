Exponentiable morphisms for a clan

Joseph Hua

Joint work with: Reid Barton

Many categories, such as Top and Cat, are not locally Cartesian closed, but still have certain exponentiable morphisms [1, 2]. We propose a more fine-grained condition of being exponentiable relative to a clan [5].

A clan (𝒞,ℛ) consists of a category 𝒞 with a class of maps ℛ whose members are called ℛ-maps, such that pullbacks of ℛ-maps along all morphisms exist and are ℛ-maps, all isomorphisms are in ℛ, and ℛ is closed under composition. Modifying the original definition in [5], we do not require that 𝒞 has a terminal object, nor that maps to the terminal object are ℛ-maps. Following [5], we use ℛ⁢(X) to denote the full subcategory of the slice 𝒞/X consisting of objects whose underlying map is in ℛ.

Fix a clan (𝒞,ℛ) and f:Y→X a morphism in 𝒞 with all pullbacks, and such that for any pullback f′:Y′→X′ of f, the restricted pullback functor (f′)∗:ℛ⁢(X′)→ℛ⁢(Y′) has a right adjoint f∗′:ℛ⁢(Y′)→ℛ⁢(X′). Theorem 1.

The following are equivalent. (i) The Beck–Chevalley condition holds at all pullbacks of f. (i⁢i) The partial right adjoint condition holds at all pullbacks of f. (i⁢i⁢i) The partial right adjoint condition holds at f and the pushforward functor (in presheaves) Πf:𝒞^/Y→𝒞^/X preserves (Yoneda images of) ℛ-maps.

The map f:Y→X is called ℛ-exponentiable if it satisfies the equivalent conditions in Theorem 1. Note that f itself need not belong to ℛ. When 𝒞 has finite limits, this notion is a special case of properness with respect to a fibration over 𝒞 [6], namely the fibration corresponding to the indexed category ℛ⁢(−). A prototypical example is 𝒞=Top and ℛ the class of local homeomorphisms, so that ℛ⁢(X)≃Sh⁢(X). Then the proper base change theorem [3] says that proper maps are ℛ-exponentiable. An example of similar flavor: in 𝒞=Cat, discrete fibrations are exponentiable with respect to the clan structure of discrete opfibrations (and vice versa). A different flavor of example is given by the category of simplicial sets with ℛ the class of Kan fibrations: the ℛ-exponentiable maps are exactly the sharp maps of Rezk [4].
