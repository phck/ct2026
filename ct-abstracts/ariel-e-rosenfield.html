Hyperstructures as models of suplattice-enriched algebraic theories

Ariel E. Rosenfield

Hyperstructures are generalizations of algebraic structures such as monoids, groups, and rings where a given binary operation is allowed to take multiple values. More precisely, a hyperoperation on a set X is a function ⋆:X×X→𝒫(X), perhaps subject to some constraints encoding associativity, unitality, et cetera, in which case we call (X,⋆) a hyperstructure. These occur naturally in the study of number theory [1], algebraic geometry [2] [4], and matroid theory [3]. The authors of [5] give constructive proofs of bicompleteness for the categories of hypermagmas, hypermonoids, mosaics, and unital and commutative subcategories thereof. In the present work, we realize these classes of hyperstructures as categories of models of suplattice-enriched algebraic theories, leading to alternative proofs of bicompleteness of these categories. Toward understanding hypergroups, we also investigate properties of the category 𝗁𝖧𝗈𝗉𝖿, whose objects are those hyperstructures corresponding to Hopf monoid objects in the category of suplattices via the adjunction 𝖲𝖾𝗍⁢(Xn,𝒫⁢(X))≅𝖲𝗎𝗉𝖫𝖺𝗍⁢(𝒫⁢(X)⊗n,𝒫⁢(X)).
