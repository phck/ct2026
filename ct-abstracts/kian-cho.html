Local model structures on cubical sets

Kian Cho

Joint work with: Steve Awodey, Reid Barton

The Cartesian cube category ℂ is a non-full subcategory of the Dedekind cube category 𝔻. The inclusion functor j:ℂ→𝔻 induces an adjoint triple j!⊣j∗⊣j∗ between Cartesian and Dedekind cubical sets. These cubical sets can be endowed with interval model structures as in [1] or equivariant model structures as in [2] (although, the presence of connection in 𝔻 implies that 𝔻^𝖾𝗊𝗏 coincide with 𝔻^𝗂𝗇𝗍 [4]). Thus we can ask whether these adjunctions are Quillen between the cubical model categories.

We will focus on j∗ being left and right Quillen between 𝔻^𝗂𝗇𝗍 and ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏. We have (1) the left adjoint j! preserves terminal object, and (2) the unit 1→j∗⁢j! is a natural weak equivalence in ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏. Furthermore, in the ∞-category world, (3) the category ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏 presents 𝒮 [2] and (4) the category 𝔻^𝗂𝗇𝗍 presents an (unknown) ∞-topos 𝒳. We conclude from (1) that j∗ presents the global sections functor Γ:𝒳→𝒮 and from (2) that Δ:𝒮→𝒳 is fully faithful. This amounts to 𝒳 being a local ∞-topos, which means 𝒮 is a localization 𝒳⁢[ℬ−1], where ℬ={♭⁢X→X∣X∈𝒳} for ♭=Δ⁢Γ. Translating back to the language of model categories, we obtain a model structure on 𝔻^ which presents spaces: the left Bousfield localization of 𝔻^𝗂𝗇𝗍 at 𝔹={j!⁢j∗⁢X→X} (which presents 𝒳⁢[ℬ−1].) We call the result the local model structure on Dedekind cubical sets, 𝔻^𝗅𝗈𝖼. We identify a generating category for the local trivial cofibrations, which can be used to construct a local (locally) fibrant universe and build a cubical model of HoTT in 𝔻^𝗅𝗈𝖼.

The relationship between 𝔻^𝗂𝗇𝗍 and ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏 resembles a familiar picture involving Cartesian cubical sets with the interval model structure ℂ^𝗂𝗇𝗍 and simplicial sets with the Kan-Quillen model structure △^𝗄𝗊. The comparison functor i:△→ℂ induces a triple i!⊣i∗⊣i∗ with the restriction i∗:ℂ^→△^ being the triangulation functor, which is left and right Quillen between ℂ^𝗂𝗇𝗍 and △^𝗄𝗊 [3]. This situation also satisfies (1)–(4), which means we obtain a model structure on Cartesian cubical sets ℂ^𝗅𝗈𝖼 which presents spaces. The counits {i!⁢i∗⁢X→X} are weak equivalences in ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏 already [2]: in fact, ℂ^𝗅𝗈𝖼 coincides with ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏, giving us yet another proof that ℂ^𝖾𝗊𝗏 presents spaces.
