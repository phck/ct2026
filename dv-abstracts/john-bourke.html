Bicategorical enrichment in algebra

John Bourke

Joint work with: Soichiro Fujii

In category theory, sometimes one does not wish to work with categories per se but instead categories over a fixed base. Such concrete categories can be viewed as categories enriched in a quantoloid, a certain bicategory. Garner [2] showed this perspective is illuminating, using it to characterise topological categories as bicategory-enriched categories which are total.

In this talk, I will explain how the same enrichment is useful in algebra, where we also sometimes work over a fixed base. We will use the bicategorically-enriched perspective to show that Eilenberg-Moore categories of monads are free cocompletions of their Kleisli categories, which is false from the traditional point of view, and use this to give a nice proof of Beck’s monadicity theorem [1] as well as Linton’s theorem [3], describing algebras as presheaves on the Kleisli category satisfying a representability condition.

  • [1] Jonathan Mock Beck, The tripleableness theorem, Reprints in Theory and Applications of Categories, No. 31, 2025, pp. 1–11.
  • [2] Richard Garner, Topological functors as total categories, Theory and Applications of Categories, Vol. 29, 2014, No. 15, pp 406–421.
  • [3] Fred Linton, An outline of functorial semantics, Lecture Notes in Mathematics 80: Seminar on triples and categorical homology theory, 1966/67, Springer-Verlag Berlin.
