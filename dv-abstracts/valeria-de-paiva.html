Fibrational Semantics for Coexisting Implications

Valeria de Paiva

Joint work with: Milly Maietti, Eike Ritter

Categorical models for linear type theories combining intuitionistic and linear logic — from the ILL (Intuitionistic Linear Logic) models of Benton, Bierman, de Paiva and Hyland, through Benton’s LNL (Linear Non-Linear logic) and Barber–Plotkin’s DILL (Dual Intuitionistic Linear Logic) — are all built around symmetric monoidal adjunctions K⊣J:𝒞→𝒮, with 𝒞 cartesian closed, 𝒮 symmetric monoidal closed, and the modality ! as the engine connecting the two fragments. But there is a natural system – ILT (Intuitionistic and Linear Type Theory), also called TILL (Two Implications Linear Logic) — that takes both intuitionistic implication A→B and linear implication A⊸B as primitive and drops ! entirely. For this system, no monoidal-adjunction model exists, and its canonical categorical semantics is genuinely open.

The key idea is to linearise the fibrational semantics of the simply-typed λ-calculus: replace the cartesian fibres of the Jacobs-style comprehension category construction with symmetric monoidal closed fibres, keeping the base cartesian to model intuitionistic contexts. The resulting ILT-indexed categories — split indexed categories E:𝐁op→𝐂𝐚𝐭 with a comprehension adjunction and a right adjoint to weakening in each fibre — give a sound and complete semantics, and ILT is their internal language.

Moreover, every ILT-indexed category already contains latent exponential structure: its Grothendieck completion yields a Benton-style monoidal adjunction, but with an idempotent comonad (!!A≅!A). Adding a left adjoint between base and first fibre recovers models for DILL and LNL, and we prove equivalences between the fibrational models and the classical monoidal-adjunction models — categories of symmetric monoidal adjunctions between a cartesian closed and a symmetric monoidal closed category, with strict structure-preserving morphisms between them. Finally, the inclusion of DILL fibrational models into ILT fibrational models has a left adjoint, DILL is a conservative extension of ILT, and the types outside ILT are precisely A⊸!B and !!A.

Although the core semantic idea was presented at before [Maietti2000], the full construction — with its careful interplay of comprehension adjunctions, fibred symmetric monoidal closed structure, and the relationship between fibrational and monoidal-adjunction models — was never published. We present it here in full, as the categorical details are of independent interest and provide a firm categorical foundation for current work on linear dependent type theory.
